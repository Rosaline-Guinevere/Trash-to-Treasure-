What is Household Waste?

Household waste is the everyday trash from our homes, like food scraps and plastic. Managing it well helps protect our environment and keeps our community clean.

See What's Happening

Why It Matters

Managing household waste is important because it protects our health, keeps our environment clean, and saves natural resources. When waste is handled the right way, less trash goes to landfills, pollution is reduced, and materials can be reused to make new products. Every small action at home adds up to a big change for our community and the planet.

Protect Nature

Proper waste management keeps rivers, soil, and air clean for future generations.

Save Resources

Recycling and reusing reduce the need for new raw materials and energy.

Healthy Communities

Less waste means cleaner streets, safer living spaces, and better public health.

Types of Household Waste

Organic

Food scraps, garden waste, biodegradable materials that can be composted into new life.

Recyclable

Plastics, paper, glass, and metals that can be transformed into new products.

Non Recyclable

Materials that cannot be reused or recycled, requiring careful disposal. Examples include mixed plastics, contaminated packaging, and items outside recycling streams.

Hazardous

Chemicals, batteries, paints, and toxic substances that pose risks to health and the environment. Hazardous waste requires specialized handling and disposal.
